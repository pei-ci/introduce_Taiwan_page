Dadaocheng and Dihua Street

Today, centuries-old traditional shops, Taiwanese-style grocery stores, historical buildings, and innovative cultural street houses are all co-existing on Dihua Street, a lively old street.

Jade Mountain

Jade Mountain is the highest peak in Taiwan. It is an ecological protection area, which requires prior application to enter.

Taipei Botanical Garden

Founded in 1896, Taipei Botanical Garden is the first botanical garden in Taiwan. The park is full of greenery, covering an area of 8.2 hectares, and has collected more than 2,000 species of plants in Taiwan. The most famous one is the lotus pond that blooms in early summer.

Sun Moon Lake

Sun Moon Lake is the largest natural inland lake in Taiwan. It is 760 meters above sea level and surrounded by mountains.

Taroko Gorge

Taroko National Park is famous for its majestic, nearly vertical marble canyon landscape. Walking along the canyon scenery line of Liwu River, you can see the scenery of cliffs, cliffs, canyons, continuous and winding cave tunnels, Dali rock formations and streams as far as you can see.
